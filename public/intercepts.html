Intercepts

Ania Ahlborn

Genre:Horror

Published: 2019

Intercepts by T.J. Payne is a gripping psychological horror novel that explores the terrifying consequences of government experimentation on the human mind.

Joe Gerhard works at a top-secret government facility conducting disturbing experiments in sensory deprivation. The goal? To turn people into "antennas" capable of intercepting intelligence by stripping them of external stimuli. But these experiments come at a horrific cost—most subjects are driven to madness, their minds broken beyond repair.

Joe has always justified his work, believing it serves a greater purpose. But when his teenage daughter, Riley, starts experiencing terrifying hallucinations—visions of a woman in a hospital gown, screaming in agony—he realizes something is terribly wrong. Either Riley is losing her mind, or she’s somehow tapping into the tortured consciousness of the facility’s test subjects.

As Joe’s personal and professional worlds collide, he’s forced to confront the horrifying reality of what he’s been a part of. Can he stop the experiments before they destroy his family, or is the nightmare already too deep to escape?
