Archer's Voice

Mia Sheridan

Genre: Romance

Published: 2014

Archer’s Voice by Mia Sheridan is a heart-wrenching contemporary romance that tells the story of Bree Prescott, a young woman seeking refuge in the quiet town of Pelion, Maine. Haunted by a traumatic past, she hopes to find peace in the stillness of the small town. What she doesn’t expect is Archer Hale—a mysterious, silent man who lives on the fringes of society. Despite his isolation, Bree feels drawn to him, sensing that his silence hides a story no one has ever cared to hear.

As Bree and Archer grow closer, she slowly unravels the tragedy that shaped him. A childhood accident left Archer unable to speak, and years of neglect and misunderstanding turned him into a ghost in his own town. But with Bree, he begins to believe in the possibility of love and connection. As their relationship deepens, Archer learns that he is more than the silence that defines him, and Bree finds a sense of home in his presence.

However, the past refuses to stay buried. When old wounds resurface and threats emerge, Archer and Bree must fight for their love and the future they never thought they deserved. Archer’s Voice is a powerful story of healing, resilience, and the kind of love that speaks even in silence. With deeply emotional writing and unforgettable characters, Mia Sheridan delivers a novel that lingers long after the final page.
